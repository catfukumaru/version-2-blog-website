Evidence of political bias in search engines and language models before major elections. A summary

Date: 31/03/26

Introduction

This article is a summary of the paper called "Evidence of political bias in search engines and language models before major elections"

The authors did more than 4,000 search engine (SE) queries and obtained 205 LLM answers before the 2024 European Parliament and US presidential elections in the important days before the election.

Current issues with LLMS and SEs including previous papers

Search engines are assumed to be more trust worthy than LLMS but both LLMs.

SE rankings can affect voting intentions for undecided voters because it affects the party's visibility and how much people care about a problem.

LLMs issues are more than just hallucination for example, overlooking inconsistencies in facts, aligning its output to what the user thinks and amplifying biases from its training data.

Early studies on SE bias compared to each other to find the differences in their results, or used sentiment analysis or the political leaning of URL domains.

Overall other papers have mixed results, some say that the lean more to the left but others say the opposite.

There are issues with previous papers being biased to the US or not explaining what is neutrality to the authors.

Methodology

Take note that the benchmark of the authors of this paper are not uniformly distributed.

The authors of this paper studied the political bias by using the frequency in which parties or issues are mentioned. They used neutral, natural and election-related prompts. They measured the salience against external indicators(media salience, polls, prior electoral results) to obtain their newsworthiness and user demand. They used vsa bot tool that mimicked human behaviour that were deployed in five European countries - Austria, Germany, Ireland, Poland, and Portugal - and 15 US counties. The bots collected results from Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo, Yahoo, Microsoft Copilot and Chat GPT.

Results of the study

There seems to be an overall bias towards the right and issues that matters to them.
